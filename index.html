AboutMe

My name is Dario Knezevic, and as my last name might suggest, I come from Croatia. Born in Croatia, I moved to Switzerland in 2001, where I’ve since built my life and career. My journey began with an apprenticeship in hotel management, which gave me valuable experience in customer service and the hospitality industry. After completing my apprenticeship, I pursued my Matura, which laid the foundation for my current studies. Today, I’m studying Business Informatics at the BFH, a field that combines my interest in technology and business. I chose this path because I am passionate about bridging the gap between customers and developers, understanding both their needs and technical solutions. This role allows me to communicate effectively and ensure that both sides are aligned in achieving their goals. I am currently in my 6th semester, focusing on Software Architecture and Design, where I’m developing the skills to design and structure complex software systems. Outside of my academic and professional life, I have a variety of hobbies that keep me active and inspired. I’ve been playing the guitar for many years, which allows me to express my creativity. Traveling is another big passion of mine, as it broadens my perspective and fuels my curiosity about the world. I also love the thrill of riding motorcycles, and enjoy staying active through sports like tennis and golf. These hobbies not only provide me with relaxation but also teach me valuable lessons in discipline, focus, and adaptability. I’m constantly looking for new challenges and opportunities to grow, and I believe my background in both business and technology positions me well to contribute to innovative projects. Whether it's through my studies, hobbies, or future career, I am excited about the opportunities ahead.
